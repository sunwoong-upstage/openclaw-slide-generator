# Layout Patterns

Use these patterns when planning the deck structure.

## Core deck arcs

### Teaching / explainer deck
1. Title
2. Goal / agenda
3. Why this matters
4. Core concept
5. How it works
6. Example or case
7. Common mistake or misconception
8. Summary
9. Q&A / closing

### Executive update deck
1. Title
2. Executive summary
3. Current status
4. Key metrics
5. Risks and blockers
6. Recommendation
7. Next steps

### Strategy / proposal deck
1. Title
2. Problem / opportunity
3. Why now
4. Options
5. Recommendation
6. Expected impact
7. Roadmap
8. Ask / decision

## Mapping content to slide types

- Use `title` for opening slide only.
- Use `heading` for section breaks sparingly.
- Use `bullet` for concise lists and takeaways.
- Use `two_col` when comparing two ideas or a concept vs example.
- Use `table` for compact trend comparisons, not dense raw data.
- Use `quote` for one memorable line or definition.
- Use `closing` for summary and action-oriented ending.

## Compression rules

When the requested duration is short:

- 5 min: 6 to 10 slides
- 10 min: 10 to 16 slides
- 15 min: 12 to 20 slides
- 30 min: 18 to 30 slides

If the deck exceeds the range, merge:
- adjacent heading + bullet pairs
- repetitive example slides
- low-value scene-setting slides
